Kebab

This kebab is often served with traditional accompaniments like flatbread, saffron rice, grilled tomatoes, and fresh herbs. It's a popular dish at gatherings and celebrations, showcasing the rich culinary heritage of Iran. Enjoy the delightful taste of Iranian Koobideh Kebab with friends and family!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef or lamb
  • 1 large onion, grated
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon ground sumac
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil (for brushing)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Meat Mixture: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground meat, grated onion, egg, sumac, turmeric, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix the ingredients thoroughly until well combined.
  2. Marinate the Mixture (Optional): For enhanced flavor, cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the mixture marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1-2 hours or overnight.
  3. Shape the Kebabs: Preheat your grill or oven to medium-high heat. Take a handful of the meat mixture and shape it around metal skewers, forming elongated kebabs. Ensure the meat is evenly distributed on the skewers.
  4. Cooking: Grill the kebabs on the preheated grill or in the oven for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally. The kebabs should be cooked through and have a nicely charred exterior.
  5. Brush with Olive Oil: While grilling, brush the kebabs with olive oil to keep them moist and add a delicious flavor.
  6. Serve: Once the Koobideh Kebabs are cooked to perfection, remove them from the grill or oven. Serve the kebabs on a plate, and you can optionally garnish with fresh herbs or a squeeze of lemon.
