Tiramisu

Tiramisu, meaning "pick-me-up" in Italian, is a classic dessert that lives up to its name. Layers of delicate ladyfingers soaked in coffee and liqueur mingle with a luscious mascarpone cream, creating a symphony of textures and flavors. It's rich yet light, creamy yet refreshing, and guaranteed to leave you wanting more.

Here's how to whip up this Italian masterpiece in your own kitchen:

Ingredients

  • 16 ounces mascarpone cheese, at room temperature
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 1 1/2 cups strong brewed coffee, at room temperature
  • 3 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional, like Kahlua or Tia Maria)
  • 40 ladyfingers (Savoiardi)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting

Steps

  1. Make the cream: In a large bowl, whisk together the mascarpone cheese and sugar until smooth. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and vanilla extract until pale and thick. Gradually whisk the egg yolks into the mascarpone mixture.
  2. Whip the heavy cream: In a clean bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until just combined. Set aside.
  3. Assemble the tiramisu: In a shallow dish, mix the coffee with the liqueur (if using). Quickly dip each ladyfinger in the coffee mixture, coating both sides lightly but not letting it soak.
  4. Arrange the ladyfingers: Arrange the dipped ladyfingers in a single layer in a baking dish.
  5. Layering: Spread half of the mascarpone cream over the ladyfingers. Top with another layer of dipped ladyfingers. Spread the remaining cream over the second layer.
  6. Chill: Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and the tiramisu to set.
  7. Dust with Cocoa: Before serving, dust generously with unsweetened cocoa powder. Sift it through a fine-mesh strainer for an even dusting.
