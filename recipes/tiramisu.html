Tiramisu

Tiramisu, meaning "pick-me-up" in Italian, is a classic dessert that lives up to its name. Layers of delicate ladyfingers soaked in coffee and liqueur mingle with a luscious mascarpone cream, creating a symphony of textures and flavors. It's rich yet light, creamy yet refreshing, and guaranteed to leave you wanting more.

Here's how to whip up this Italian masterpiece in your own kitchen:

Ingredients

  • 16 ounces mascarpone cheese, at room temperature
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream, cold
  • 1 1/2 cups strong brewed coffee, at room temperature
  • 3 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional, like Kahlua or Tia Maria)
  • 40 ladyfingers (Savoiardi)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting
